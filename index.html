Our Community

An Indigenous Community Between Two Volcanoes

Los Ramos is a small indigenous community on Ometepe Island, a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve in Lake Nicaragua. Our village sits 16 kilometers south of the port town of Moyogalpa, on an ancient Pre-Columbian cemetery between the two volcanoes that form this island. Ometepe sees thousands of visitors every year, but most of that money ends up at resorts and businesses owned by expats. Very little reaches the indigenous communities that actually live here.

Years ago, two young Naturalists from Yosemite saw an opportunity: what if visitors came directly to our community? That vision has come back to life. Every dollar you spend here goes directly to local families — not to middlemen, not to hotel chains. We believe travel is about more than staying at a resort and checking a destination off your list. It is about sitting at a family's table, learning to milk a cow, walking the farm with a farmer. This is daily life in Los Ramos, and we invite you to be part of it.

Bird Banding Experience

Spend a full day with local bird researchers — learning to identify species, observing birds up close, and participating in hands-on bird banding in the forests of Ometepe.

Learn More

Ometepe Island is home to an extraordinary diversity of bird species, and this experience puts you right in the middle of it. You'll join local researchers at their forest banding station, learning how they carefully capture, measure, band, and release birds as part of ongoing conservation efforts. Throughout the day you'll observe dozens of species in their natural habitat, learn to identify them by sight and sound, and gain a deeper understanding of the island's ecosystems. A home-cooked meal is included, shared with the researchers and your fellow visitors.

Frequently Asked

Common Questions

Quick answers to the questions we hear most often. Something else on your mind? Email us at losramoscommunity@gmail.com.

Do I have to stay overnight to book a program?

No. Every program is bookable as a standalone visit. Homestays are optional — many visitors come for a single cooking class or horseback tour, while others stay for a week.

How much does a homestay cost?

$10 per room per night, plus $15 per day for three home-cooked meals. Every dollar goes directly to the host family.

Is payment cash only?

Yes. No deposit is required when you book — you pay in cash (USD or Nicaraguan córdoba) when you arrive in Los Ramos.

How do I get to Los Ramos from Managua?

Fly into Managua (MGA), take a bus or shuttle about 2.5 hours south to San Jorge in Rivas, catch the ferry to Moyogalpa on Ometepe Island, then travel 16 km south to Los Ramos. We can help arrange the last leg — just email us when you book.

What language do the guides speak?

Most community members speak Spanish. A bilingual member of the community leads the Spanish lessons and can help with English-speaking guests on tours.

Can children join the programs?

Yes. Cooking class, milking tour, kayaking, and theater are all family-friendly. Email us when you book and mention how old the kids are so we can plan accordingly.
